Exercise 1-Multiple Monitors

Note: you are welcome to use the multiple monitors in Somsen 301 whenever the classroom is available.

Your HP laptop can support two external monitors through VGA and DisplayPort cables (G2 or G3); or HDMI and USB-C Thunderbolt to DisplayPort cables (x360); or USB-C Dock (x360 only) .

A MacBook Pro can use multiple monitors, the best solution is to use a CalDigit TS3+ hub in Somsen 301 or 329.
Why are additional screens necessary? Some reasons:

  • Conveniently run multiple software applications on separate monitors
  • Easily copy and paste data between open applications
  • Participate in Adobe Connect web conference while also running other applications
  • Watch class recordings while also running other applications
  • Be able to keep Outlook open in one monitor at all times

To setup, use and disconnect an external monitor from your laptop follow the instructions below.

Somsen 301 is equipped with dual external LCD monitors for the instructor and each of the students to increase productivity.  This arrangement permits the display of several applications on each monitor. External monitors promote the use of multiple applications such as video conferencing, web browsers and eBooks.  An eBook can be displayed on one monitor, a spreadsheet application is displayed on another and a web video conferencing session is on the third monitor.  The mouse will move freely and smoothly between monitors that are correctly oriented.

Three monitors, outlined in red, orange and blue.

Note: the Intel HD Graphics Control Panel looks slightly different on the x360.

When using LCD projectors or video conferencing screen sharing you can have a ‘public’ and ‘private’ display. The primary laptop display can be private, viewable only by the user. The external LCD monitor and the ceiling mounted LCD projector both display the same ‘public’ image. This allows the user to view class notes, email, Desire2Learn information privately on their laptop. Note that the user can still use their laptop and LCD projector to display the same image as is done now, and disregard the external LCD monitor. This is known as ‘mirroring’.

  1. Connect the VGA cable from the left-side monitor that is in 'portrait' mode to the VGA port on the left-side of your laptop.
  2. Connect the DisplayPort cable from the right-side monitor to the DisplayPort on the right-side of your laptop.
  3. Click the 'Start' button and type 'Intel HD Graphics', then start the Intel HD Graphics Control Panel application.
  4. Choose 'Display' and you will be presented with a dialog box that allows you to adjust the rotation of each monitor. Note that:
    1. "Monitor 1" must be your laptop display
    2. "Monitor 2" should be your landscape display
    3. "Monitor 3" should be your portrait display
  5. In the top left in 'Select Display' choose the third monitor in the list.
  6. In the center section near the bottom select '90' for the 'Rotation'
  7. Click 'Apply' in the lower right.
Individual display settings

Multiple display settings- need to take another screen shot in Somsen 301

  1. As necessary drag your monitors into the correct physical location as depicted in the 'Arrange Displays' section in the right side of the dialog box. Then click apply. Accept the changes.
  2. To test that you have configured your monitors correctly you should be able to move the Graphics Control Panel dialog box between all three monitors seamlessly by clicking and dragging on the dialog box title bar. Repeat the above steps as necessary until you can do this.
  3. Close the dialog box.

Exercise 6-Subscribe to Outlook Calendars
Required:  All of the assignments for this course are listed in the D2L calendar. You can also view the assignment due dates in your Outlook desktop client by subscribing to the feed provided by D2L. Follow these steps so that you have all of your assignment due dates for this course available.
  1. Log into D2L and load this course. i>Click on 'Calendar' or 'Materials>Calendar' from the menu.

  2. Click on the 'Settings' icon. (also note the Subscribe icons, you will use this later)

  3. Make sure to check 'Enable Calendar Feeds"

  4. Click the Subscribe button (see Step 3) and select your course from the list, then copy the calendar feed, and paste it into Outlook.


  5. Open Outlook Desktop, choose File>Account Settings>Account Settings...   When the dialog box appears choose the "Internet Calendars" tab, click "New..."  Paste the D2L calendar feed url, click "Add", use the course name as the folder name, click OK, then click close.

  6. It will take several seconds to install, then your Outlook Calendar will appear with the new calendar feed for this course.
    You may need to adjust your calendar settings to view the due dates properly.
    'Week' view is shown below instead of the typical 'Work Week' view so that you can see the Saturday assignment due dates.

  7. Repeat as desired to add calendars from other D2L courses.
