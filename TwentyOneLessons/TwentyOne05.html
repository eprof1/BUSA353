Technological advances are changing our world at a rate never before experienced.

The twin revolutions in information technology and biotechnology confront us with the biggest challenges our species has ever encountered.
How do you live in an age of bewilderment, when the old stories have collapsed and no new story has yet emerged to replace them?

19. (50) Education

In the past humans suffered from a lack of information and education.
Today we try to make sense of too much information, and misinformation.
The goal is to be able to tell the difference between what is important and what is not.
It is important that this generation has a comprehensive view of the cosmos to guide rational decision making.
Important skills are critical thinking, communication, collaboration, creativity; and dealing with change.


What will be the result when people choose not to become educated, especially about themselves?

20. (50) Meaning

From the beginning humans have told stories to explain the individual's place and the meaning of life.
These stories have been used by religions, nations, Disney, communists and idelogical movements.
A good strategy is to consider the purpose these stories serve.


If you want to know the truth about the universe, the meaning of life and your identity; how do you start?

21. (50) Meditation

Meditation is an attempt to know and exert control over one's mind. This is a difficult task.


What is at risk if we collectively fail to decide who we are?
